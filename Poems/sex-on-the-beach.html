"Sex on the Beach"

The crowd was a womb.
The liquor was brown like the Coke, like the night.
The pen was a neon star in that sky
But glowed softly like a child's reading light beneath her blankets
And scrawled stories in the cosmic smoke –
Stories that lifted the Earth on a breeze.
The murmuring ebbed and flowed overhead.
I dipped in,
but just a toe.
the air like salt stung my nostrils and warmed my throat.
And my stone shoes were secured,
Though I didn't remember putting them on.
The space above was hemmed with silver,
And the continental margin was steep.
And I closed my eyes to the rushing waves.

The murmuring calmed, trapped in a bubble,
And I began to perspire
like some black sun had warmed the waters for a thousand years.
My feet threw me into a lover's embrace,
and I blinked and I had slept for years
Pressed against the fleshy air.
“I'm okay.”
And I am borne into the sunlight.

This is an upturned mouth,
Slumped shoulders, a deep breath
From the bench on the sidewalk
On the glittering shoreline
Where my hair and shirt sleeves wave
Like palm leaves
and my skin dimples
