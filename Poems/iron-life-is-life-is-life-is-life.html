Iron (Life Is Life Is Life Is Life)

La Buena Vida was
before Death selfishly died for my sake.

I hung in Her humid embrace.
Rather, She hung in mine
between the hours of 12:13 and 2:37 AM.
For those endless few hours
no sound echoed in her hollow frame
nor did any in mine.
Pools of flesh coalesced on the ground
as if poured from the ethereal pitcher of moon.
The vase of her irises frosted over,
and spiders leapt from the roots
on woven threads of daggers,
tugged by the sisters.
The earth’s writhing fingers reached for them,
plucking them from the melting sky
and built new life from them.

Mala Vida—
Darkness that brings sunshine
on amethyst days when lilac clouds fill the skies
and piercing frozen storms when
the fields are barren and opal
and glistening shadows meld with the night air.
A haze of rotten meat hangs over the ground
as warmth overtakes this tundra
and summons the earth’s organic tendrils
to grope for new life
and pulse with resurrection.
Her name is Mala.
She is the Angel of Death, black and full of Vida.

She clasps hands of cold copper over my own eyes
to preserve them for Charon.
Payment to continue living.
But Charon desires the crown,
As we all do,
of a kingdom that once was—
tall and ominous, regal and proud—
hung in white banners, olive branches,
and sweet cinnamon.
But the throne is cold, the court empty,
and the halls are hung with dust
and acrid mildew.
The crown is no longer golden,
but tarnished iron,
because the gold was only a phantom—
an illusion.

Charon envisions gold because he envies life,
but life envies Death,
because Her copper fingers shall join
with the golden earth’s and be reshaped
into new life—Vida Buena.
