A Devil's Night Disco Nap

The flames climb higher, those that reach deeper
to set alight the explosives of my desire;
the effect is something akin to a tire fire.
Noxious fumes fill my ego balloon,
ready to explode with demon rage;
the sound of a "POP" that drops the load
of pressure and stones, heavy weight
that bore down on my twig-ish bones
as they cracked, muscles frozen and stiff,
supported alone. The fire heats them,
tickles them red with O2, rejuvenates,
reinvigorates--let's life back in
through the thin cracks in the fibers,
breathing up the sweet asbestos smoke
that chokes Detroit out like poverty
and abandoned Ford, Chrysler, GM factories.
One needs rest before the club scene,
a disco nap to take the edge off
before the ecstasy looses its trap
and the little neon lights become
roaring beacons in the night,
dancing flames flashing bright
and police sirens like fog horns--
ships yearning for the cove.
My rage settles with the ash
risen to the stage, a starlet
burning out, bright Betty White
flames fighting to the last
flailing ember as it sizzles
into Lake Erie to die,
Cleveland it's last sight.
