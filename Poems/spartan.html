Spartan

I am Spartan, grip tight on gilded spear stained hot once-blue,
Whose running lays the sun to rest in rich and fertile pools

That reek of rotting oak and smelted iron soaking down;
Black flies suspended like shouting fog above the ground.

I am Spartan with shield of burned and beaten tortoise shell
That sounds of thunder clap against aged Liberty Bell,

An amber beacon in red-clay fields flashing through dust cloud--
Buried sense in acrid decay, I taste the death of pride.

I am Spartan--tall, taut, muddied--my flesh a pitted sword
Hewn by spilled sweat, hardened by unceasing labors of war.

Our blades clash like oiled cogs, gearing our hearts for beating
That marches on like pounding feet; each strike is deafening.

I am Spartan--ashen face and bared teeth--muscles tight, strong
For the call that sounds the charge like victory's siren song.

I am Spartan, scars my only take from fights never won
For empires chewing conquest and spewing honest bones.

I am Spartan who will not eat blindly, but will instead,
Tread upon the silver-streaked stones my brothers have been fed.

I am Spartan, I fight for family, honor, and country,
For the gaunt-sinewed strength of men, both in thought and will free.

I am Spartan, I will not lie down like the waning moon;
With my brothers I take up arms until I too, am blue.
