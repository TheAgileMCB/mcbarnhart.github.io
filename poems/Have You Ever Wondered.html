Have you ever wondered what the inside of a stomach looks like?

By Matthew Barnhart

I want to understand.
I hope they find something—nothing serious, just “something.”
I’m hungry.
Have you ever wondered what the inside of a stomach looks like?

The ceiling stares back at me with stern lips.
The pressed sheet of its surface is stark and blank.
It whispers warnings of “good luck.”
The blankets are warm, but the eyes are sterile and cold.
They just stare downward, bright and unblinking,
not caring to follow me as I pass.
I think of bags of chips and pretzels, tins of popcorn
as the gurney rattles and clunks below me.

God, I’m hungry.

The procedure room is as uncaring as the faces surrounding me.
They smile, but they won’t feed me
anything but twilight.

Twilight. What is that? Where is that? Where am I?
Jesus, I’m hungry.

“Good luck.”

Jesus…?

Choking. I think.
At least… I think I think.

Choking, coughing, sputtering.
Where am I?
Sunlight stings my eyes.
I want to understand.
What did the doctors say?
Nothing?
Crying.

“Don’t worry; the medication elicits a very emotional response from teenagers.”

Daddy, don’t leave me.

The sunlight tickles my nose.
I don’t understand.
I remember I don’t understand.
Crying.

“I’m not going anywhere.”

I’m not going anywhere.

“They’ll figure it out.”

I’m not going anywhere.

Sunlight bathes my feet.
The room has more color, more furniture—
A plant, a T.V., and a nightstand in the corner.
The blankets are warm, but the air in my nostrils is still sterile and cold.

What did the doctors say?
Nothing?
Nothing…
They’ll figure it out.
Can I see the pictures?

“Sure, baby.”

Daddy, I’m hungry.

“We’ll ask the nurses.”
