Shakespeare, Dante, Milton, and John Lennon

Shakespeare made well-known the English sonnet
But wasn’t much for the pew or the hymns.
He wrote no shortage of brilliance and wit,
Twisting his words like wind twists willow limbs.
Milton recounted current events, grim
As politics, death, and his birthdays had,
But lost sight of the world, like grey fog, sad
And opaque, before it lost sight of him.
Dante Alighieri, nearest the birth,
Dabbled in cliché before it was so.
Of fair ladies’ love and sweetness he wrote,
Damning them to an inferno of mirth.
Lennon was damned at too early an age.
There’s no counting the lives he could have saved.
